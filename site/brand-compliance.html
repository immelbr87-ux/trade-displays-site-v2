Brand compliance

Pilot-friendly guidelines to keep listings clear, factual, and brand-safe.

Why this exists

Showrooms often sell floor models and clearance items. This page clarifies how to do that in a brand-safe, professional way during the pilot.

Default guidelines (recommended)

  • Use as-is language consistently.
  • Do not imply manufacturer warranty unless explicitly confirmed for that item.
  • Avoid using brand logos in listing photos unless they’re naturally part of the product.
  • Describe condition with observable facts (scratches, missing trim) rather than opinions.

Safe listing language (copy/paste)

Condition note: “This is a showroom display item sold as-is. Buyer will inspect at pickup prior to acceptance. Normal display wear may be present as disclosed in photos and notes.”

Warranty note: “Manufacturer warranty is not guaranteed for display/clearance items unless explicitly stated by the showroom.”

Pickup note: “Pickup-only. Buyer is responsible for transport and any required equipment/labor.”
