Brand compliance (pilot defaults)

Trade Displays reduces exposure and misrepresentation risk by presenting specs, condition, and pickup timing first. Listings are explicitly “showroom display (installed previously)” and avoid “new/warranty” language.

Request pilot access Open checklist PDF View example listing

Listing rules (what we do)

  • Specs-first: dimensions, rough-in, finish family, completeness, grade
  • Display disclosure: installed previously (showroom display)
  • Pickup window: shown and enforced
  • Final sale: designed around pickup inspection + confirmation

What we don’t do

  • No brand “blowout” framing or public discount marketing language
  • No consumer-to-showroom negotiation threads
  • No implied warranty / certification / “like new” beyond grade definition
Showroom control Your store decides what brand identifiers appear on listings. Pilot default: minimize brand exposure and keep listings specs-first.

Condition grades (used consistently)

These definitions show up the same way across browse, details, and pickup confirmation.

Grade A

Light wear, fully functional, all core components present.

  • Minor scuffs / small marks
  • No missing core pieces
  • Ready-to-install condition expected

Grade B

Visible wear and/or minor non-critical parts variance (disclosed).

  • Visible scratches / finish wear
  • Minor replacement part may be needed
  • Still functional; details shown in listing
Pilot defaults are for risk reduction. Final program language should be reviewed with internal policies and agreements.
