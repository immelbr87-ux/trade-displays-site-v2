Brand compliance (pilot defaults)

Trade Displays is designed to reduce brand exposure and misrepresentation risk. Listings prioritize the information trade buyers actually need: dimensions, rough-in, finish family, functional completeness, condition grading, and pickup timing.

Listing rules (what we do)

  • Specs-first: dimensions, rough-in, finish family, completeness, and grade are shown upfront.
  • No warranty language: listings do not imply manufacturer warranty or “new” status.
  • Display disclosure: items are clearly described as showroom displays (installed previously).
  • Final sale: no returns or exchanges.
  • Local pickup: pickup window is shown and enforced.

What we don’t do

  • No public “blowout” framing or marketing language that implies discounting the brand.
  • No consumer-to-showroom direct messaging or negotiation.
  • No claims about warranty, certification, or “like new” beyond the grade definition.
Showroom control Your store can decide whether brand identifiers are shown on listings. The pilot default is “specs-first” and professional-only access.
