FAQ

Clear answers to the most common questions from showroom teams and trade buyers.

Basics

What is Trade Displays?

Trade Displays is a pickup-only listing and transaction flow designed for showrooms to move floor models and clearance items with clear buyer expectations. Buyers inspect at pickup and confirm acceptance before funds are released.

Is this a marketplace with delivery?

No. Trade Displays is pickup-only. We do not coordinate shipping or delivery.

Who is it for?

Showroom teams that occasionally remove or replace displays (vanities, faucets, toilets, fixtures) and want a clean, documented way to sell them as-is without post-pickup disputes.

Pickup, inspection, and payment

When does the buyer pay?

At reservation. Funds are held until pickup is completed and the buyer confirms the item matches the listing.

What happens at pickup?

The buyer inspects the item on-site. If it matches the listing, the buyer confirms acceptance and funds are released.

What if the item doesn’t match the listing?

The buyer can decline acceptance at pickup. In that case, the transaction is cancelled and funds are not released.

Condition and disputes

What do Grade A and Grade B mean?

Grade A is showroom-ready with only minor wear (light scuffs, normal display handling). Grade B has noticeable cosmetic wear or missing non-critical items that are clearly disclosed. Full definitions are on the Trust & safety page.

Are returns allowed?

After the buyer accepts at pickup, the sale is final. Items are sold as-is.

What disputes are allowed?

Disputes are limited to material misrepresentation (e.g., incorrect model, undisclosed major damage, missing critical components when the listing said “complete”). Normal wear consistent with the grade is not dispute-eligible.

Using this with leadership / compliance

How do we summarize results internally?

Completed sales can be summarized by count, gross sales volume, average sale price, and dispute count. This is designed to be easily forwardable to leadership.

Does this change showroom warranty policy?

No. These are as-is sales of display/clearance items. Any manufacturer warranty policies remain unchanged and should be communicated consistently in the listing.
