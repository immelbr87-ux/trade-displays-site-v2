How Trade Displays works

Trade Displays helps showroom teams move floor models without the usual headache — with clear condition disclosure, scheduled pickup windows, and funds released only after buyer acceptance at pickup.

The flow (end-to-end)

1

List

Create a listing in under a minute using a showroom-friendly template (category, dimensions, condition, pickup window).

2

Reserve

A buyer commits at a fixed price. Funds are held in escrow while the pickup is scheduled.

3

Pickup + inspection

Buyer inspects at pickup. If the item matches the listing, they accept on-site.

4

Confirm + release

Once accepted, funds are released automatically. The sale is recorded in a simple internal summary.

Finality at pickup: No delivery, no returns after acceptance, and no post-pickup disputes. Once the buyer accepts on site, the sale is final and liability transfers to the buyer.

For Sellers (Showrooms)

Showrooms constantly struggle with stale inventory that takes up valuable floor space. Trade Displays digitizes a pickup-only liquidation workflow so teams can move floor models quickly, with cleaner protection and less admin.

Why showrooms would be interested (the “push”)

  • The shipping headache: Heavy, unboxed floor models often require custom crating and expensive freight, and damage claims are common. Pickup-only removes the biggest logistics pain point.
  • Cash flow & space: Showrooms need to cycle new collections in. Email blasts and warehouse sales are administratively heavy. “List in 1 minute” is attractive if it actually brings a buyer.
  • Protection: Escrow protects sellers from post-pickup disputes. Once the buyer accepts at pickup, the deal is done.

What sellers get operationally

  • Specs-first listings with clear condition grades
  • Scheduled pickup windows with dock notes
  • Fixed pricing (no haggling)
  • On-site acceptance → sales final after acceptance

For Buyers (Trade)

Interior designers, stagers, and architects often need high-end items fast and under budget. Floor models can be available today — and inspection at pickup makes “as-is” purchases far less risky.

Why trade buyers would be interested (the “pull”)

  • Instant gratification: Lead times for new high-end product can be 12–20+ weeks. A floor model is available now — crucial for tight project timelines.
  • Risk mitigation: The #1 fear with “as-is” online is receiving more damage than expected. Inspect-at-pickup lets a buyer reserve confidently and refuse only if the condition is materially worse than described.
  • Margin: Floor models can sell for 50–70% off, enabling savings for clients or healthier trade margin.

The gap this fills

Today this is manual:

  1. Call a rep.
  2. Ask: “Do you have any floor models of X?”
  3. Drive to the showroom to see it.
  4. Haggle.

Trade Displays digitizes this specific local liquidation workflow — reserve, inspect, accept, and release funds.

What’s included

  • Pickup-only transactions (no shipping)
  • Buyer inspection before acceptance
  • Funds held until buyer acceptance
  • Clear condition standards and category templates
  • Simple sales summary for leadership visibility

What’s not included

  • No manufacturer warranty language
  • No “new” status claims
  • No delivery, shipping, or install coordination
  • No buyer negotiations or back-and-forth
  • No system integrations required for a pilot

Transaction safeguards

Buyer expectations

  • Items are sold as-is, as described
  • Bring a vehicle and materials needed for transport
  • Inspect at pickup before accepting

Disputes (edge cases)

  • Disputes are limited to material misrepresentation vs the listing
  • Normal wear consistent with Grade A/B is not a dispute
  • After acceptance at pickup, sales are final
