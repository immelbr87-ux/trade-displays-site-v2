Brand-Safe Pilot Overview

This page is intended to share how the pilot is structured to be brand-safe and operationally simple for showrooms. The pilot is focused on installed showroom displays scheduled for removal and sold to verified trade buyers.

Key idea Listings are specs-first (dimensions, finish family, condition A/B, pickup window) and avoid public brand identifiers.

How the pilot stays brand-safe

  • Specs-first public listings — no public brand names, SKUs, or model numbers.
  • No warranty representation — installed displays are sold as-is.
  • Trade-only access — verification is required to reserve.
  • Controlled timing — pickup windows are shown upfront and enforced.

Operational controls (to reduce issues)

  • Fixed pricing set by the showroom (no bidding).
  • Escrow held until pickup confirmation.
  • Condition confirmed at pickup using Grade A / Grade B.

What the pilot is (and isn’t)

  • Is: a structured trade workflow for upcoming display removals.
  • Is not: public retail clearance, consumer resale, or manufacturer buyback pricing.
